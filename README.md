## 📖 About the Project

This website is developed as part of the **Humanities course — "The Role of Technology in Development"** at **IIT Jodhpur**. It investigates how the Indira Gandhi Canal Project (IGNP), one of the world's largest canal systems, played a foundational role in making IIT Jodhpur feasible as an institution in the arid Thar Desert region of Rajasthan.

### Research Cluster: IIT Jodhpur (Cluster B)

The project addresses the following core research questions:

1. **What role did the Indira Gandhi Canal play in making IIT Jodhpur feasible as an institution in this location?**
   - Infrastructural dependencies assumed during campus planning
   - Institutionalization and bureaucratic mediation of water supply to IIT
   - Engineering perspectives on the risk of canal failure

2. **How do students, faculty, and staff perceive (or not perceive) canal-linked water?**
   - Moments when water becomes visible in daily campus life
   - When water disappears from collective attention
   - Assumptions the campus community holds about water supply
